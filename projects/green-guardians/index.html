Product Challenge

Making Environmental Literacy Actionable

Environmental education can feel abstract unless learners can connect it to real places, choices, and community action. Green Guardians needed a product system that could make climate, ecosystems, conservation, and sustainability more visual, approachable, and classroom-ready.

The platform also needed to connect teachers to a growing network of community-based partners and programs — turning environmental education from a solo classroom effort into a community-connected learning experience.

Global Competition

Global Climate Literacy Competition

Green Guardians also hosted the Global Climate Literacy Competition, a digital, games-based climate learning competition for students. The competition turned climate literacy into short, repeatable micro-games with progress, questions, competition mechanics, and participation data.

The first Global Climate Literacy Competition engaged 3,300+ students from 174 schools across 37 countries, challenging Grades 6–8 learners through micro-games related to climate change, green energy, and water.
