Product Challenge

Meena is one of UNICEF South Asia's most recognizable educational characters. For this project, the challenge was to reintroduce Meena for a new generation of children through stories about emotional wellbeing and mental health — while producing the series efficiently across multiple languages and regional contexts.

The project required more than visual redesign. It needed a scalable production system that could support 6 episodes, 4 language versions, localized voiceover, lip-sync adaptation, social content, and consistent character performance across all deliverables.

I led the visual and animation production pipeline, combining hands-on design execution with workflow design, AI-assisted asset generation, and modular puppet animation systems to make the production faster, more consistent, and easier to scale.

My Role & Ownership

I owned the end-to-end visual production pipeline for the series, working from early visual direction through final multilingual delivery. Design System for Characters

Redesigning Meena and the supporting cast required balancing cultural familiarity, emotional warmth, and production scalability.

The original references were limited and inconsistent, so I rebuilt the cast into a cleaner, more unified character system. Each character was designed to preserve cultural identity while supporting repeatable animation, multilingual lip-sync, modular mouth shapes, hand poses, and AI-assisted production workflows.

This created a reusable visual system that improved consistency across episodes and made future adaptation easier.

Scalable Animation System

To support 6 episodes across 4 languages, I rebuilt the characters as modular puppet rigs with reusable mouth shapes, hand poses, and expression systems.

This allowed the same character assets to be re-performed for different language versions while preserving consistency in timing, emotion, and visual quality. The system reduced repeated manual work and helped keep production efficient across a multilingual delivery pipeline.

The result was a scalable animation framework that balanced expressive storytelling with production speed.

AI-Assisted Workflow

AI was not used as a visual shortcut, but as a production accelerator. I integrated AI tools into specific parts of the workflow where they could improve speed without compromising control, consistency, or emotional quality.

AI was used primarily for background exploration, prop generation, environment variations, and selected video moments such as flashbacks or complex movement references. These outputs were then manually refined in Photoshop and integrated into the animation pipeline.

This approach reduced repetitive production effort, accelerated asset creation, and allowed more time to focus on character performance, storytelling clarity, and localization quality.
